## 1. Data We Collect

### 1.1 Information you give us

- **Account data:** name, email, password (hashed), company name, role, country, billing address, phone number, language preference.
- **Billing data:** processed by **PayPal**; we receive transaction IDs, subscription status, and limited card metadata (last 4, brand, expiration). We do not store full card numbers.
- **User Content:** brand assets, copy, prompts, lead/contact lists, integrations data, voice scripts, audio recordings (RevTry), templates, customizations.
- **Communications:** messages to support, abuse reports, survey responses.

### 1.2 Information we collect automatically

- **Usage data:** features used, prompts submitted, models invoked, tokens consumed, voice minutes, pages published, send volumes, errors, performance metrics.
- **Device and connection data:** IP address, browser, OS, device identifiers, timezone, referrer URL, language.
- **Cookies and similar technologies:** see Section 13.
- **Audit logs:** authentication events, admin actions, publish events, acknowledgments (see `05e`), API calls, security events.

### 1.3 Information from third parties

- **Identity / fraud signals** from PayPal and fraud-prevention vendors.
- **Enrichment** (optional, configurable): firmographic data from vetted providers, only when enabled.
- **Integrations data:** when you connect a Third-Party Integration, we receive data per your authorization (e.g., contacts from a CRM, send results from an email/SMS gateway).

### 1.4 Sensitive / special-category data

We do not intentionally collect special-category data (e.g., health, biometrics, sexual orientation, political opinions, religion, precise geolocation, government IDs, children's data). **Do not** upload such data via User Content unless you have a lawful basis and we have agreed in writing (e.g., a signed Data Processing Addendum and any required additional terms).

### 1.5 AI training data

We do **not** use your User Content or Generated Content to train foundation models without your separate, opt-in consent. Aggregated, de-identified usage telemetry may be used to improve the Service.

## 7. Retention

We retain personal data only as long as needed for the purposes described in this Policy and to meet legal obligations.

| Category | Retention |
|---|---|
| **Account data (profile, settings, configurations)** | Lifetime of the Account, then **deleted within 90 days** of cancellation (backup expiry) |
| **User Content** | Lifetime of the Account; export available for 30 days after cancellation, then deleted within 90 days of cancellation |
| **Billing records, invoices, tax records** | Retained as required by law (typically **7–10 years**) |
| **Audit logs (auth, admin actions, publish events, regulated-vertical acknowledgments)** | **7 years** |
| **Security and abuse logs** | Up to **24 months** |
| **Support tickets** | **3 years** after closure |
| **Marketing prospect data (where lawful)** | Until consent withdrawn or 24 months of inactivity |
| **Backups** | Rolling encrypted backups; production deletions propagate to backups within **90 days** |
| **Legal holds** | Longer where required by litigation, regulation, or investigation |

After retention expires, data is deleted or irreversibly anonymized.

## 10. Security

We implement administrative, technical, and organizational measures appropriate to the risk, including:

- **Encryption in transit** (TLS 1.2+) and **at rest** (AES-256 or equivalent).
- **Access controls** (least privilege, MFA for staff, role-based access).
- **Network segmentation, secrets management, key rotation.**
- **Vulnerability management, dependency scanning, periodic penetration testing.**
- **Logging and monitoring** for anomalous activity.
- **Backups** with tested restore procedures.
- **Vendor risk management** and DPAs with subprocessors.
- **Personnel training, background checks where lawful, confidentiality obligations.**
- **Incident response plan** with defined roles and escalation paths.

No system is perfectly secure. You are responsible for safeguarding your credentials.

## 12. AI and Automated Processing Disclosure

12.1 **AI-generated outputs.** The Service uses generative AI models (large language models, image, audio, and voice models) to produce drafts. Outputs are statistical predictions; they can be inaccurate, biased, or non-compliant. You are responsible for review and compliance — see `05a` Section 5.

12.2 **Automated decisions.** The Service performs automated processing such as:

- **Content moderation** to detect AUP violations;
- **Fraud and abuse signals** to protect the Service;
- **Voice-agent script routing** in RevTry;
- **Anti-spam classifiers** on outbound content.

12.3 **No solely automated significant decisions about individuals.** We do not make decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects on an individual based solely on automated processing without human involvement. Where you configure the Service to make automated decisions about your own end-users (e.g., lead scoring, eligibility), **you are responsible for transparency, human-review rights, and bias controls** under GDPR Art. 22, LGPD Art. 20, and equivalent laws.

12.4 **Right to explanation / human review.** If you are subject to an automated decision by us, contact `privacy@gofunnelai.com` to request human review.

12.5 **Voice recordings (RevTry).** Calls placed or received by the voice agent may be recorded and transcribed. You are responsible for obtaining any consent required by **two-party consent** laws (e.g., California, Florida, Illinois, Washington, EU member states, Brazil) and for providing call-recording disclosures.
