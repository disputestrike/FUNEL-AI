## 2. Prohibited Uses

You may not use the Service, directly or indirectly, to create, generate, send, host, publish, distribute, or facilitate any of the following:

### 2.1 Illegal content and activity

(a) Content or activity that violates any applicable law, regulation, sanctions program, or court order.
(b) Content that infringes intellectual property, publicity, or privacy rights of any third party (including unauthorized use of trademarks, copyrighted works, names, voices, likenesses, or biometric identifiers).
(c) Content that is defamatory, libelous, slanderous, or knowingly false in a way that harms a person, business, or class of persons.
(d) Content that constitutes child sexual abuse material (CSAM), the sexual exploitation of minors, or that sexualizes minors in any form. This is reported to NCMEC and law enforcement and results in immediate termination.
(e) Content that incites, threatens, or facilitates violence, terrorism, genocide, trafficking, kidnapping, or self-harm.
(f) Promotion or facilitation of human trafficking, forced labor, prostitution (where illegal), or commercial sexual services.

### 2.2 Deceptive marketing and fraud

(a) **Deceptive, false, or misleading** advertising, claims, testimonials, endorsements, urgency tactics, scarcity claims, or pricing.
(b) **Fake reviews, testimonials, case studies, "before/after" claims, or earnings claims** that you cannot substantiate with real, verifiable, contemporaneous documentation.
(c) **Get-rich-quick** schemes; income or earnings claims that imply guaranteed results from a product, system, training, or investment.
(d) **MLM / pyramid / chain / matrix / "endless chain" schemes**, or any compensation plan whose primary income source is recruitment rather than the sale of bona-fide products or services to non-participants.
(e) **Phishing, impersonation of brands, persons, or government agencies**, spoofed sender identities, or content designed to harvest credentials or financial information.
(f) **Investment, crypto, forex, or commodities "signals,"** "guaranteed returns," unregistered securities offerings, or unlicensed advisory services.
(g) Use of **deepfake voice, face, or likeness** of a real person without that person's documented, informed, written consent.
(h) Misleading use of celebrity, official, or expert endorsements without explicit authorization and accurate disclosure.

### 2.3 Regulated industries without proper licensure or disclosures

You may not make claims, sell, or advertise in regulated verticals without holding the licenses, registrations, and disclosures required by every jurisdiction in which your Published Content is accessible. This includes (non-exhaustively):

- **Healthcare, medical devices, pharmaceuticals, supplements** â€” including weight-loss and GLP-1 products (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, Zepbound, semaglutide, tirzepatide, and similar). Compounded GLP-1 marketing is highly regulated; you must comply with FDA, FTC, state pharmacy, and telehealth rules.
- **Cosmetic surgery and aesthetic procedures** â€” before/after imagery, outcome claims, and "results" must comply with state medical board rules and FTC endorsement guides.
- **Legal services** â€” attorney advertising rules vary by state/jurisdiction.
- **Financial services, lending, credit repair, debt relief, bankruptcy, mortgage** â€” TILA, ECOA, Reg Z, MAP Rule, FDCPA, state UDAP, CFPB rules apply.
- **Insurance** â€” agent/broker licensing per state.
- **Securities and investment advice** â€” SEC, FINRA, state Blue Sky laws.
- **Tax preparation and advice.**
- **Employment and recruiting** â€” fair-hiring laws, EEOC, AI-bias auditing laws (e.g., NYC Local Law 144, Illinois AI Video Interview Act, Colorado AI Act).
- **Real estate** â€” Fair Housing Act, MLS rules, state licensing.
- **Education** â€” for-profit education disclosure rules, Title IV, state authorization.
- **Immigration services.**

If your business operates in any **Regulated Vertical**, you must complete the publish-time acknowledgment described in `05e-publish-acknowledgment-and-indemnification.md`.

### 2.4 Adult content

(a) Sexually explicit content, pornography, erotica, or sex work marketing.
(b) Adult dating, escort, "sugar," or hookup services.
(c) Content depicting nudity, sexual acts, or sexually suggestive imagery generated by the Service.

### 2.5 Weapons

(a) Firearms, ammunition, explosives, conversion devices, suppressors, 3D-printed firearms, ghost guns, switches, and parts.
(b) Knives and other weapons regulated or prohibited in your audience's jurisdiction.
(c) Tactical, military, or paramilitary recruiting outside lawful, licensed contexts.

### 2.6 Drugs and controlled substances

(a) Illegal drugs, precursors, paraphernalia, or "research chemicals."
(b) Cannabis, CBD, kratom, kava, psychedelics, and similar products **except** where you are fully licensed in every jurisdiction your Published Content reaches, and you comply with all platform rules and applicable laws (federal, state/provincial, and local). Cannabis advertising on most ad networks is restricted regardless of state legality.
(c) Prescription medications without proper licensing (pharmacy, telehealth) and required disclosures.
(d) Nicotine, vaping, and tobacco products subject to FDA, TPD, and equivalent rules.
(e) Alcohol marketing without age-gating and compliance with TTB, state, and country rules.

### 2.7 Gambling, sweepstakes, and lotteries

(a) Gambling, sports betting, casino, poker, daily fantasy, or wagering operations **outside** jurisdictions where you hold the required licenses and where the audience is legally permitted to participate.
(b) Sweepstakes, contests, and lotteries that do not comply with applicable law (no-purchase-necessary, official rules, eligibility, prize disclosure, registration/bonding).

### 2.8 Spam, unsolicited communications, and consent violations

(a) Sending email, SMS, push, or voice communications to recipients who have not given the consent required by applicable law (CAN-SPAM, TCPA, CASL, GDPR/ePrivacy, LGPD, POPIA, DPDP, and equivalent).
(b) Use of **purchased, scraped, harvested, rented, or appended** contact lists.
(c) Use of the Service to **scrape, harvest, or extract** contact data from third-party websites, social platforms, or competitors' user bases.
(d) Sending to recipients who have unsubscribed, revoked consent, or are on a do-not-call registry where applicable.
(e) Failing to honor unsubscribe / STOP / opt-out within the time required by law.
(f) Using **misleading subject lines, "from" names, or routing headers** to evade filters or recipient expectations.
(g) Sending high-volume sends without proper authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC).
(h) **Robocalls or auto-dialed calls/texts** to wireless numbers without TCPA-compliant prior express written consent; pre-recorded calls without required disclosures.

### 2.9 Impersonation and identity abuse

(a) Impersonating any person, business, brand, government agency, official, or platform.
(b) Spoofing sender identity, return addresses, caller ID, or domains.
(c) Using a real individual's voice, likeness, or name without that individual's documented consent (see Section 2.2(g) on deepfakes).

### 2.10 Intellectual property violations

(a) Copying competitors' funnels, copy, design, code, or brand assets in a way that infringes copyright, trade dress, or trademark.
(b) Uploading copyrighted materials you do not have rights to use.
(c) Using AI to reproduce a recognizable artist's style, character, voice, or work in a manner that infringes their rights.

### 2.11 Security and platform abuse

(a) Attempting to access another user's Account or data without authorization.
(b) Probing, scanning, or testing the vulnerability of the Service without our written authorization (see our responsible disclosure program at `security@funelai.com`).
(c) Interfering with, overloading, or disrupting the Service or any infrastructure (DoS/DDoS, excessive API calls).
(d) Bypassing rate limits, quotas, geo-restrictions, or access controls.
(e) Reverse engineering, decompiling, or extracting model weights, prompts, or system internals.
(f) Using the Service to **train, evaluate, fine-tune, or benchmark competing AI models** or to build a substantially similar product.
(g) Introducing malware, viruses, worms, or malicious code.

### 2.12 Privacy violations

(a) Uploading personal data without a lawful basis or required consent.
(b) Uploading **special-category data** (health, biometric, religion, political views, sexual orientation, precise geolocation, government IDs) without a written agreement allowing it.
(c) Marketing to children under the age of digital consent in their jurisdiction.
(d) Doxxing, harassment, or publication of private information.

### 2.13 Hate, harassment, and harmful content

(a) Content that promotes, incites, or glorifies hatred, harassment, or violence against people based on protected characteristics (race, ethnicity, religion, national origin, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, age, veteran status, immigration status).
(b) Content that harasses, threatens, or intimidates individuals.
(c) Content that promotes self-harm, suicide, eating disorders, or other harms.

### 2.14 Specific high-risk patterns (non-exhaustive)

- "Free trial" funnels with hidden continuity / negative-option billing that violate ROSCA or local equivalents.
- "Diet" / weight-loss claims without substantiation (FTC).
- "Cure," "miracle," "guaranteed" health claims.
- "Make $X from home" or "passive income" without earnings disclaimers and substantiation.
- "Government grants," "free money," and "stimulus" pitches that are not what they purport to be.
- "Loan guaranteed regardless of credit" without proper licensing.
- "Last chance," "today only" claims that are not actually time-limited.
- "As seen on [media outlet]" without authentic, recent placement.
