## 1. Market Overview

US family law services market is $14.2B in 2025, growing 2–4% YoY [industry estimate based on ABA + Statista 2025]. Approximately 770,000 divorces filed per year (declining slightly from 1M pre-pandemic). Plus modifications, custody, paternity, prenups, and mediations. Average matter fee: $3,500 uncontested divorce / $8,500–$24,000 contested no-kids / $18,000–$95,000+ contested with kids and assets; $1,200–$3,800 prenup; $4,500–$22,000 mediation; $4,800 custody modification.

Sales cycle: 2–14 days for retainer signing. Gross margin: 38–52% after associate / paralegal time. LTV: typically single matter but modification revenue is real (~$3k–$8k every 3–5 yrs). Acceptable CAC: $400–$1,400. Acceptable CPL: $42–$220 at 8–18% close.

Buying triggers: (1) decision to file for divorce; (2) served with divorce papers (urgent); (3) custody dispute escalation; (4) abuse / DV emergency; (5) engagement → prenup; (6) child support modification (income change); (7) relocation request; (8) DCF / CPS involvement.

## 2. Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: Initiating Divorce (38%).** 32–55, married 7–22 years, $80k–$280k HHI, often with kids. JTBD: "Understand process; protect kids; protect assets; not get screwed." Verbatim: "I want this to be amicable but I need protection." Fear: losing custody, losing house, getting drained legally. Winning: free consult, transparent retainer.

**Persona 2: Responding to Divorce (24%).** Just got served. Urgent. JTBD: "Respond before default; protect rights." Winning: same-day intake.

**Persona 3: Custody Dispute (16%).** Already divorced or never married. JTBD: "Modification / enforcement." Fear: judge bias. Winning: prior win record with judges in that county.

**Persona 4: High-Asset Strategic (12%).** $500k+ HHI, business owner, complex assets. JTBD: "Asset protection / business valuation." Winning: forensic accountant network.

**Persona 5: Engagement / Prenup (10%).** 25–55, pre-wedding, often re-marriage. JTBD: "Protect assets; clarity." Winning: streamlined turnaround.

## 12. Compliance Rules

**ABA Model Rules 7.1–7.5 + state analogues:** truthful, no specialist claim without certification. **Solicitation Rule 7.3:** no live solicitation of represented parties; no direct contact with adverse parties. **State Bar advertising filing:** FL Bar 4-7.21 pre-filing; TX 7.04; KY ad filing; NV. **Trust Account (IOLTA):** retainer goes to trust; cannot be marketed as "$0 down" if trust deposit required. **Confidentiality (Rule 1.6) + privilege:** intake forms must NOT funnel through unencrypted third-parties or pixel-tracked pages. **TCPA / CAN-SPAM / FTC §5.** **State family law statutes** govern outcomes (TX Family Code, FL Statutes Ch 61, CA Family Code, NY DRL). **DV laws** state-specific (VAWA federal + state DV statutes). **UCCJEA** (Uniform Child Custody Jurisdiction and Enforcement Act). **Conflict checks** mandatory. **ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA.**
