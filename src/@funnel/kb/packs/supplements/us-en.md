## 11. Prohibited Claims (the regulated heart of this pack)

DSHEA permits structure/function claims (e.g., "supports immune function") but PROHIBITS disease claims (e.g., "treats COVID," "cures cancer," "prevents Alzheimer's"). Any disease claim turns the product into an unapproved drug under FDA. FTC has filed actions for unsubstantiated weight-loss and cognitive claims. Influencer testimonials need typical-results disclosure. "Clinically proven" requires actual trials.

## 12. Compliance Rules

**DSHEA (Public Law 103-417, 21 USC §321(ff)):** dietary supplement definition; structure/function claims permissible with FDA disclaimer "This statement has not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease." This disclaimer is REQUIRED on every structure/function claim. **FDA NDI (New Dietary Ingredient) notification (21 CFR §190.6).** **21 CFR §111 cGMP** for manufacturing. **FDA labeling (21 CFR §101)** — Supplement Facts panel + ingredient declaration + allergen labeling. **FTC Act §5 + 2022 Health Products Compliance Guidance:** substantiation = competent and reliable scientific evidence (often RCTs). **FTC Endorsement Guides (16 CFR §255).** **FTC ROSCA + Click-to-Cancel (2024).** **CAN-SPAM / TCPA.** **CPSC** for product safety. **Prop 65** for listed substances. **State labeling laws** (CA, NY). **NAD / BBB** ad challenges. **DEA** for any scheduled substances. **State Pharmacy Board** if state-specific. **ADA / WCAG 2.1 AA.** Recent FDA + FTC enforcement against NMN, DMAA, kratom, SARMs, certain peptides. Bacopa, ashwagandha, rhodiola — adaptogen claims must be S/F not disease.
