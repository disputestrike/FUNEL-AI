## 1. Market Overview

US retail wealth advice market managed approximately $36T in client assets in 2025 across 14,000+ RIAs and 280,000 advisors (RIA + B/D combined) [industry estimate based on Cerulli + IAA + FINRA 2025]. Average fee revenue per advisor: $480k–$1.4M. Average AUM per advisor: $90M–$220M, with HENRYs (high earners not rich yet) and pre-retirees the dominant target. Average new-client value: $1.2M AUM → $12,000 first-year fee at 1.00% blended.

Sales cycle: 60–180 days from first touch to AUM transfer due to trust-building, financial-plan delivery, and legacy account paperwork. LTV: 9.4-year average client retention → $112,000 per acquired client. Acceptable CAC: $1,200–$4,800 per onboarded client. Acceptable CPL: $48–$220 at 6–12% lead-to-onboarded.

Buying triggers: (1) job change with 401(k) rollover; (2) retirement / pension lump-sum; (3) inheritance; (4) sale of business / liquidity event; (5) divorce; (6) widowhood; (7) cross-border move; (8) RMD age (73 under SECURE 2.0); (9) tax-planning need; (10) Roth conversion / IRMAA cliff awareness.

## 2. Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: Pre-Retiree 55–65 (38%).** $200k–$700k HHI, $500k–$5M assets, 3–10 yr to retirement. JTBD: "Can I retire? When? What's my number?" Verbatim: "I want a Monte Carlo run." Fear: outliving money. Winning: written financial plan, Roth/Social-Security/Medicare strategy.

**Persona 2: 401(k) Rollover (22%).** Just left job. JTBD: "Where do I roll this?" Winning: fiduciary advice, low-cost portfolio.

**Persona 3: Business Sale Liquidity Event (12%).** Just sold biz $2M–$50M. JTBD: "Manage the proceeds, minimize tax, estate plan." Winning: CFP/CFA + estate attorney network.

**Persona 4: Widow / Divorcee (10%).** Often woman 50–70, sudden control of finances. JTBD: "Trustworthy advice; education." Winning: empathy + CDFA designation (divorce) or CTFA (widow).

**Persona 5: Cross-Border / Expat (8%).** US/Canada or US/UK dual. JTBD: "Tax-efficient cross-border."

**Persona 6: HENRY (10%).** 32–45, $250k+ HHI, $200k–$1M assets. JTBD: "Tax-efficient wealth build, ESPP/RSU, real estate."

## 11. Prohibited Claims

Banned by SEC Marketing Rule (Rule 206(4)-1, effective Nov 2022): testimonials/endorsements require specific disclosures including compensation, material conflicts, and disclosures about whether the person is a client. Performance advertising requires net of fees, prescribed time periods (1, 5, 10 yr), no cherry-picking, no past-specific-recommendations without listing all in same class. **Cannot guarantee returns. Cannot use SIPC/FDIC mark for securities. Cannot claim "registered" as a quality endorsement.**

**Hard-block exact strings:**
- `"guaranteed returns"`
- `"guaranteed [X]% per year"`
- `"risk-free investment"`
- `"FDIC insured"` (only for bank products)

## 12. Compliance Rules

**Investment Advisers Act of 1940 (15 USC §80b):** RIA registration with SEC or state (under or over $100M AUM). **Reg BI (Regulation Best Interest, 17 CFR §240.15l-1):** broker-dealer obligation. **SEC Marketing Rule (Rule 206(4)-1, 2022):** comprehensive rewrite of advisor advertising rules including testimonials/endorsements, performance advertising, predecessor performance, third-party ratings (Barron's, Forbes — must disclose criteria + compensation). **Form ADV** annual update + delivery. **Form CRS** to retail customers. **Custody Rule.** **Anti-fraud (§206(1)–(4)).** **State Investment Adviser Act + state-by-state notice filings.** **FINRA Rule 2210** for B/D communications. **TCPA / CAN-SPAM / FTC §5.** **Reg S-P (Privacy of consumer financial info).** **Reg S-ID (Identity Theft).** **CCPA / state privacy.** **State Insurance Department for annuity sales.** **ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA.** **Senior Investor protections (NASAA model).**

## 22. Pitfalls

(1) Testimonials without SEC Marketing Rule disclosures. (2) Past-specific recs without listing all. (3) Performance without net-of-fees. (4) Third-party rating (Barron's) without disclosing criteria + compensation. (5) "Registered with SEC" implying endorsement. (6) Operating outside state registration. (7) Form CRS not delivered. (8) ADV not annually updated. (9) Promising returns. (10) Cold-call against state DNC + TCPA.
