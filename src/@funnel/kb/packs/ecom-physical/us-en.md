# GoFunnelAI KB Pack — E-Commerce Physical Goods (DTC) (US-EN)

> Pack ID: `ecom-physical-us-en` | Vertical: DTC physical goods (apparel, home, beauty non-supp, electronics, accessories) | Geo: US | Language: EN

## 1. Market Overview

US DTC physical goods e-commerce is $620B in 2025 (~22% of total US retail), growing 8–12% YoY [industry estimate based on Census + Statista + Shopify 2025]. Average DTC brand profile: $100k–$80M annual revenue; AOV $58–$148; gross margin 55–72%; first-purchase repeat rate 22–38%. Average ticket: $42 single SKU, $98 bundle, $180 multi-item.

Sales cycle: 0–14 days first-touch to purchase. LTV: 1.4× AOV (average); 3.2× AOV (top-quartile). Acceptable CAC: $14–$58 (paid social). Acceptable CPL: $2–$18 (email opt-in) at 12–28% lead-to-purchase.

Buying triggers: (1) seasonal (Q4 BFCM, spring); (2) social discovery (TikTok / Instagram); (3) influencer trigger; (4) replenishment cycle (consumables); (5) gift occasion; (6) competitor disappointment; (7) lifestyle change. Heavy creative-iteration / Meta ad-platform dependent.

## 11. Prohibited Claims

Banned: "Made in USA" without FTC §323 substantiation (all or virtually all from US); "biodegradable" / "eco-friendly" without FTC Green Guides substantiation; before/afters or weight-loss promises (apparel, beauty); cosmetic claims for non-drug products that imply drug-level effects (FDA); "guaranteed lowest price" without basis; fake countdown timers; "as seen on Shark Tank" without basis.

**Hard-block exact strings:**
- `"Made in USA"` (without FTC §323 substantiation context)
- `"100% American made"` (same)
- `"eco-friendly"` (without specific Green Guides-compliant claim)
- `"biodegradable"` (without substantiation + decomposition timeframe)
- `"compostable"` (without specific home-compost or industrial-compost qualifier)
- `"recyclable"` (without disclosure if facilities limited)
- `"sustainable"` (without specific claim)
- `"non-toxic"` (without basis)
- `"chemical free"` (false — water is a chemical)
- `"organic"` (without USDA NOP)
- `"hypoallergenic"` (FDA: no standard)
- `"as seen on Shark Tank"` (without actual appearance)
- `"as seen on TV"` (without basis)
- `"FDA approved"` (without basis; FDA doesn't approve most cosmetics/apparel)
- `"clinically proven"` (without trial)
- `"#1 in [category]"` (without basis)
- `"guaranteed lowest price"`
- `"only 3 left in stock"` (if fake)
- `"price doubles tomorrow"` (false)
- `"limited edition"` (if not actually limited)

## 12. Compliance Rules

**FTC Act §5:** substantiate every product claim. **FTC Made in USA Rule (16 CFR §323):** "all or virtually all" — substantial misrepresentation actionable. **FTC Green Guides (16 CFR §260):** sustainability/eco claims must be specific and substantiated. **FTC Endorsement Guides (16 CFR §255):** influencer #ad; testimonial typical-results disclosure. **CPSC** for product safety; **CPSIA** for children's products (lead, phthalates, tracking labels). **California Prop 65** warning labels for listed substances. **FCC Part 15** for electronics. **FDA cosmetic labeling (21 CFR §701)** for beauty (cosmetic vs OTC drug line). **NAD / BBB ad challenges.** **CAN-SPAM** for email; **TCPA** for SMS marketing (express written consent required). **PCI-DSS** for payments. **GDPR / CCPA / CPRA.** **FTC ROSCA + Click-to-Cancel** for subscribe-save. **State sales tax nexus (post-Wayfair).** **Section 5 Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act** for warranty claims. **ADA / WCAG 2.1 AA** required for online retail (DOJ position + active litigation).
