## 1. Market Overview

US residential roofing is a $24.7B annual market in 2025, growing ~6% YoY driven by aging housing stock (median US home age is 43 years) and increased storm frequency [industry estimate based on IBISWorld + NRCA 2025]. Approximately 5.0M residential roof replacements occur annually plus another 8M repairs. Average deal size for a full re-roof is $11,800 nationally — ranging from $7,500 (asphalt 3-tab, small ranch) to $42,000+ (metal standing seam, complex cut-up roof). Specialty work (slate, tile, premium architectural shingles) reaches $80,000–$160,000. Gross margin per deal typically 35–48% on materials/labor for asphalt, 22–32% on metal, lower on insurance-claim work where supplements drive margin.

Sales cycle: median 11 days first-touch to signed contract for non-storm work; 4–7 days for insurance claim work where adjuster has already approved. Buyer LTV is modest (~1.2 transactions per lifetime on the same property) but referral economics are exceptional — every closed roof produces an average 1.8 neighbor referrals in 24 months. Acceptable CAC: $400–$900 per closed deal. Acceptable CPL: $35–$95 for marketing leads converting at 8–14%.

Buying triggers: (1) visible storm damage (hail, wind, fallen tree); (2) active leak; (3) home insurance non-renewal threat over roof age; (4) home sale prep (inspector flagged); (5) refinance appraisal flagged roof; (6) HOA notice of violation; (7) neighbor just replaced (social proof + canvasser activity); (8) end of asphalt warranty period (15–25 years post-install); (9) energy efficiency / cool roof tax credit; (10) post-hurricane FORTIFIED Roof incentive (FL, AL, NC, SC, TX). TAM per regional contractor: $4M–$30M depending on storm season. SOM in year 1 via GoFunnelAI: $1.2M–$6M typical.

## 2. Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: Storm-Damage Homeowner (42%).** 35–65, single-family, $80k–$180k HHI, in a hail-belt state (TX, CO, OK, KS, NE) or hurricane coast (FL, NC, SC, LA, AL). JTBD: "Get my roof replaced through insurance with minimal stress and zero out-of-pocket beyond my deductible." Verbatim: "the adjuster said it was just cosmetic," "my deductible is $2,500 — can you help with that?" Fear: getting denied by carrier or stuck with a chasing contractor. Winning: contractor handles adjuster meeting, supplements approved, full replacement covered, only deductible due. **Disqualifier:** contractor offering to "waive your deductible" — this is insurance fraud in most states.

**Persona 2: Aged-Roof Replacer (28%).** 45–70, homeowner 15–25 years, $70k–$200k HHI. JTBD: "Replace my aging roof before it leaks and before my insurance drops me." Fear: insurance non-renewal letter, ceiling stain. Winning: financed deal, architectural shingle upgrade, transferable warranty. Verbatim: "Travelers just sent me a non-renewal."

**Persona 3: Active-Leak Emergency (14%).** Any age, urgent. JTBD: "Stop the leak today." Fear: structural / mold damage. Winning: same-day tarp + scheduled replacement.

**Persona 4: Home-Sale-Prep Seller (11%).** Listing within 60 days, told by realtor or inspector to replace or credit. Fear: deal falling through. Winning: fast turnaround, written warranty transferable to buyer.

**Persona 5: Premium-Upgrade Owner (5%).** $400k+ HHI, building permanent home, wants metal/slate/tile, aesthetic-driven. Fear: bad workmanship. Winning: portfolio of premium installs.

## 4. Urgency Triggers

Seasonal: (1) Post-storm 14-day window before adjuster timelines close; (2) Insurance renewal cycle (carriers send non-renewals 30/60/90 days pre-renewal); (3) Pre-hurricane / pre-winter season (Aug–Sept Gulf; Sep–Oct Northeast). Financial: (4) New deductible structures (% of dwelling rather than flat); (5) FORTIFIED Roof rebates (AL up to $10k); (6) Cool roof energy tax credit. Regulatory: (7) FL SB-76 statute of limitations on claims (1 year + 1 year to supplement, per 2024 reform); (8) State AOB (Assignment of Benefits) reforms; (9) Local code changes requiring ice/water shield extensions, drip edge, ventilation calcs.

## 5. Common Objections

- **O1 — "My adjuster said only one slope is damaged."** Rebuttal: "Often adjusters miss matching-color discontinuation under state matching statutes — let's request reinspection."
- **O2 — "I need 3 quotes."** Rebuttal: "Smart. While you compare, can I send you our written warranty and proof of license, bond, manufacturer cert? Most quotes don't include those."
- **O3 — "Can you waive my deductible?"** Rebuttal: "No — that's insurance fraud in [state]. But I can show you financing for it."
- **O4 — "Your price is higher than the storm chaser."** Rebuttal: "They use 3-tab and skip code-required underlayment. Here's the line-item difference."
- **O5 — "I want to wait until spring."** Rebuttal: "Spring is hailstorm season here — and our schedule fills up. If your roof is damaged now, every rain compounds it."
- **O6 — "I don't trust roofers."** Rebuttal: "Fair. We give you the lead installer's cell, daily photos, and a no-leak written guarantee."
- **O7 — "My HOA has rules."** Rebuttal: "We've installed in [HOA name] 12 times. Send me your CC&Rs and I'll match to compliant shingles."
- **O8 — "I'm worried about the deck rot."** Rebuttal: "We replace any rotten decking at $X/sheet, documented with photos, with your approval before re-decking."
- **O9 — "What about manufacturer warranty?"** Rebuttal: "We're [Owens Corning Platinum / GAF Master Elite / CertainTeed Select ShingleMaster] which is required for the [50-year non-prorated] warranty."
- **O10 — "I want a metal roof, but it's too expensive."** Rebuttal: "Initial cost is 2.4× asphalt but 50-year life vs 22 — let me run the lifetime cost."

## 11. Prohibited Claims

Banned: (a) "free roof" except where genuinely free of all cost; (b) "we'll cover/eat/waive your deductible" — felony insurance fraud in most states; (c) "guaranteed insurance approval"; (d) "lifetime warranty" without disclosing transfer/proration terms; (e) "FEMA pays for your roof"; (f) "government grant for roof replacement" (no such federal grant exists for individual homeowners outside specific disaster declarations); (g) "no out of pocket" if customer has any deductible.

## 12. Compliance Rules

**TCPA (47 USC §227):** Form opt-in must obtain explicit one-to-one consent (FCC 2025). Quiet hours 8 a.m.–9 p.m. local. SMS STOP/HELP. **FTC Act §5:** Substantiate every claim. **State licensing:** TX no statewide; FL CCC; CA C-39; OH no state but local. License # on every ad. **Insurance fraud statutes:** Every state criminalizes deductible-rebating; most explicit (FL §817.234; TX Ins. §707.0511; NC §58-33-85). **AOB law:** FL SB-76 (2021) and SB-2A (2022) restrict assignment of benefits to roofers. **State matching statutes:** vary; cite state-specific (e.g., FL §626.9744, IA §515.137). **Storm-chasing statutes:** many states require 3-day cooling-off (e.g., TX §27.01 of Bus & Comm Code) post-disaster. **CAN-SPAM:** standard. **ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA.**

## 22. Pitfalls

(1) Promising to waive deductible — felony in most states. (2) Door-knocking same day as storm — regulated in most states. (3) Buying shared storm leads from networks (one-to-one TCPA risk). (4) Not pulling permits — state license suspension. (5) Allowing salesperson to negotiate adjuster (only licensed public adjusters can). (6) Letting RevTry call delay >5 min. (7) Not requiring address on form — burns ad spend on out-of-area leads. (8) Vague "lifetime warranty" claims without proration. (9) Skipping ventilation calcs and code upgrades. (10) Using stock drone footage on testimonial pages.
