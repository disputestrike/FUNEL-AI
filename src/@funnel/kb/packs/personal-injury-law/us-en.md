# GoFunnelAI KB Pack — Personal Injury Law (US-EN)

> Pack ID: `personal-injury-law-us-en` | Vertical: Plaintiff's personal injury (MVA, premises, product, workers' comp adjacent) | Geo: US | Language: EN

## 1. Market Overview

US plaintiff personal injury legal services is a $57B annual fee market in 2025 (third-party fees, not gross settlements), growing 4–6% YoY [industry estimate based on ABA + Statista + Thomson Reuters 2025]. Approximately 49,000 active plaintiff PI firms; roughly 22M annual ER visits for injury + 6M annual MVA crashes drive intake. Average case fee revenue: $14,800 soft-tissue MVA; $42,000 moderate injury w/surgery; $180,000+ catastrophic; $850,000+ wrongful death; $25k–$50k typical premises slip-and-fall; product liability and mass tort widely variable.

Sales cycle: 5 min to 7 days first-touch to signed retainer (intake speed is everything — slowest firms lose 50%+). Gross margin per case after costs, expert fees, and treatment liens: 38–55%. LTV: largely single-case but referral economics excellent. Acceptable CAC: $400–$1,800 per signed retainer. Acceptable CPL: $35–$220 per signed-lead; $14–$58 per intake form fill.

Buying triggers: (1) MVA in last 7 days; (2) injured at work (workers' comp + third-party); (3) slip-and-fall; (4) dog bite; (5) medical malpractice (high-value, complex intake); (6) wrongful death; (7) defective product / mass tort consumer (Roundup, talc, hair relaxer, social media MDL); (8) DUI victim (separate from defense). High-velocity intake.

## 2. Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: Recent MVA Victim (52%).** 25–65, just in a crash, urgent. JTBD: "Make sense of what to do; get medical care; get someone to deal with insurance." Verbatim: "The other guy ran a red light." Fear: signing away rights, missing deadlines, getting lowballed by insurance. Winning: free consult, contingency only, treating doctor referral, 24/7 intake.

**Persona 2: Workers' Comp Adjacent (16%).** Injured on job, third-party liability (e.g., subcontractor). JTBD: "Maximize recovery — wc + third party." Winning: dual-track expertise.

**Persona 3: Slip-and-Fall / Premises (12%).** 35–70. JTBD: "Compensation for injury, medical bills." Fear: blamed for own clumsiness.

**Persona 4: Mass Tort / MDL (10%).** Any age, exposure-based. JTBD: "Join class / MDL." High volume intake → mass tort referral firms.

**Persona 5: Catastrophic / Wrongful Death (10%).** Family of injured/deceased. JTBD: "Justice + financial security." High-stakes referral / co-counsel.

## 5. Common Objections

- **O1 — "I'm fine, I don't need a lawyer."** Rebuttal: "Injury symptoms often surface in days. Free consult locks in your options. Statute is [X yr] in [state]."
- **O2 — "The insurance offered me a check."** Rebuttal: "Initial offers average 30% of case value. Don't sign — let us review."
- **O3 — "I caused part of it."** Rebuttal: "[State] is comparative fault. You can still recover."
- **O4 — "Lawyers take 40%."** Rebuttal: "Contingency typical 33–40%; you pay nothing if we don't win. Net to you is usually higher than going alone."
- **O5 — "I don't want to go to court."** Rebuttal: "95%+ settle before trial."
- **O6 — "Statute / time?"** Rebuttal: "[State]: [X yr]. Clock started at incident date — act today."
- **O7 — "I already saw a chiropractor."** Rebuttal: "Good. Documentation matters. We coordinate with treating providers."
- **O8 — "I don't trust attorney ads."** Rebuttal: "Fair. Read our 200+ Google reviews; verify state bar standing."
- **O9 — "How long until I get money?"** Rebuttal: "Median 6–14 months; sometimes 6 weeks for clear-liability soft-tissue."
- **O10 — "What's my case worth?"** Rebuttal: "Damages = medical bills + lost wages + pain/suffering + future care. We estimate at consult."

## 11. Prohibited Claims

Banned: "guaranteed result," "we'll win your case," specific past-result claims without state-bar-required disclaimers, "best lawyer in [city]" without verifiable basis, "specialist" / "expert" if not state-bar-certified specialist (varies by state), comparative claims without basis, contingency without disclosing costs may be owed if lost (in some states), "no costs" without clarifying expert/court costs may apply.

## 12. Compliance Rules

**ABA Model Rules of Professional Conduct (and state analogues):** Rule 7.1 (truthful communications), 7.2 (advertising), 7.3 (direct contact / solicitation), 7.4 (specialist designation). **State Bar rules vary significantly:** TX TDRPC 7.04; FL Bar Rule 4-7; CA Rule 7.1–7.5; NY DR 2-101; NJ RPC 7.1. **Florida Bar advertising:** filing required for many ads; "no win, no fee" allowed with disclaimer; past results allowed only with required disclaimer. **TCPA / CAN-SPAM / FTC §5.** **Truth in Advertising:** all past-result claims must include "Past results do not guarantee future outcomes. Each case is different." **State Bar attorney advertising filing:** FL, TX, KY, NV pre-filing; others post. **Solicitation rules (Rule 7.3):** no in-person or live phone solicitation of non-clients without prior relationship (except mass tort if compliant); 30-day post-accident written solicitation ban in some states (FL, KY, MO). **ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA.** **GLBA does not apply (no consumer financial product).** **Conflict checks** required at intake.

## 22. Pitfalls

(1) "Specialist" / "Expert" without state-bar specialty (Rule 7.4 violation). (2) Past results without required disclaimer. (3) Cold solicitation within state-specific post-accident bans (FL 30-day, KY, MO). (4) Cross-state representation without admission or pro hac vice. (5) Sharing fee with non-lawyer (mass tort referral arrangements must be lawyer-to-lawyer with co-counsel agreement). (6) Pixel on intake forms — privilege/PII risk. (7) "Free" without cost disclosure. (8) Lead-network sharing violates many state bars and TCPA. (9) State-by-state filing required for ads in FL/KY/NV/TX. (10) Conflict checks skipped at intake.
