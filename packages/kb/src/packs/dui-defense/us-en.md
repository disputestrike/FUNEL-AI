## 1. Market Overview

US DUI defense services market is $5.6B in 2025 [industry estimate based on ABA + Justice Statistics 2025]. Approximately 1.1M DUI arrests per year (down from ~1.5M in 2010s due to policy/Uber). Average matter fee: $2,500 first-offense misdemeanor plea-handled; $5,800–$12,000 first-offense fight or trial; $8,500–$22,000 second offense; $15,000–$45,000 felony DUI / DUI w/injury / DUI w/death; $1,200–$3,800 administrative license hearing.

Sales cycle: 48 hours to 14 days (urgency-driven; admin license suspension clock typically 10–30 days post-arrest). Gross margin: 42–58%. LTV: largely single-case; expungement years later (+$1,500–$4,500). Acceptable CAC: $400–$1,400. Acceptable CPL: $48–$220.

Buying triggers: (1) just arrested for DUI; (2) just got DMV / administrative hearing notice; (3) ignition interlock requirement; (4) CDL holder (commercial license, federal DOT consequences); (5) under-21 zero tolerance; (6) felony enhancement (3rd+ offense, injury); (7) refused breathalyzer; (8) federal-property DUI; (9) immigration consequences for non-citizens.

## 2. Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: First-Time DUI Panic (52%).** 22–55, never been arrested, professional or middle-class. JTBD: "Make this go away with minimum life damage." Verbatim: "I had two glasses of wine." Fear: job loss, license loss, jail, family knowing. Winning: free same-day consult, fight-the-case approach with realistic plea fallback.

**Persona 2: CDL Holder DUI (12%).** Commercial driver. JTBD: "Save my CDL — even off-duty arrest disqualifies under 49 CFR §383." Winning: CDL-specialist firm.

**Persona 3: Repeat Offender (16%).** 30–55, prior conviction(s). JTBD: "Avoid felony / mandatory jail." Winning: specialty hard-fight firm.

**Persona 4: Refused Breath/Blood (10%).** Implied-consent suspension issue. JTBD: "Win at admin hearing." Winning: 10-day filing speed.

**Persona 5: Non-Citizen DUI (10%).** Immigration consequences. JTBD: "Avoid deportable conviction." Winning: padilla-compliant counsel.

## 5. Common Objections

- **O1 — "Cost?"** Rebuttal: "Flat-fee depending on plea/fight. Free consult to scope."
- **O2 — "I'll just take the plea."** Rebuttal: "Pleas have lifelong consequences. 30-min consult to understand."
- **O3 — "Public defender will do it."** Rebuttal: "PDs are great in court but overloaded. Free consult to compare."
- **O4 — "I blew over the limit. Can't be defended."** Rebuttal: "Breathalyzer calibration, traffic stop legality, blood-test chain — many defense angles."
- **O5 — "License — can I drive?"** Rebuttal: "Hardship / restricted available in most states."
- **O6 — "What about my job?"** Rebuttal: "Strategy varies by occupation. Healthcare / CDL / teacher / pilot — each different."
- **O7 — "I'm not a citizen."** Rebuttal: "Padilla-compliant immigration co-counsel."
- **O8 — "How long does this take?"** Rebuttal: "Admin hearing 30–90 days; criminal 6–18 months typical."
- **O9 — "Will it stay on my record?"** Rebuttal: "Expungement / sealing eligibility in [state] after [X yrs]."
- **O10 — "I refused the test."** Rebuttal: "Implied-consent automatic suspension; admin hearing 10-day window."

## 12. Compliance Rules

**ABA Model Rules 7.1–7.5 + state analogues:** truthful, specialist requires certification. **State Bar advertising filing:** FL/KY/NV pre-filing. **Solicitation Rule 7.3:** post-arrest direct solicitation banned in many states (FL Bar 4-7.18 30-day rule). **Past-results disclaimer required.** **TCPA / CAN-SPAM / FTC §5.** **State DUI statutes:** vary (CA VC §23152; FL §316.193; TX Penal §49.04; NY VTL §1192). **DMV / admin license rules:** state-specific; 10-day filing common. **49 CFR §383 (Federal Motor Carrier Safety):** CDL DUI consequences. **8 USC §1101(a)(43) + 1227 (immigration consequences):** Padilla advisory required for non-citizen clients. **Trust accounting (IOLTA).** **Conflict checks.** **ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA.**
