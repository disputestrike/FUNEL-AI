## 1. Market Overview

US cosmetic surgery market is $19.8B in 2025 (surgical-only; combined with med-spa $44B+), growing 6–9% YoY [industry estimate based on ASPS + ASAPS 2025]. Approximately 7,500 board-certified plastic surgeons and another ~12,000 physicians performing cosmetic procedures. Top procedures and average prices: breast augmentation $9,800; mommy makeover $24,500; tummy tuck $13,500; liposuction $7,800; rhinoplasty $12,500 (revision $18k+); BBL $14,500 (declining due to safety concerns + GLP-1 effect); facelift $22,800; deep-plane facelift $42,000; eyelid surgery $7,200; gyno male $9,500; FFS / GAS individual procedures vary.

Sales cycle: 14–120 days from first touch to surgery date due to financing and surgical scheduling. Gross margin: 35–50% per case; surgeon income highly variable based on overhead. LTV: $24,000–$48,000 per patient (often returns for second procedure). Acceptable CAC: $480–$1,800. Acceptable CPL: $58–$280.

Buying triggers: (1) major life event (divorce, weight loss, post-pregnancy); (2) anniversary / "treat myself"; (3) GLP-1 weight loss creating "Ozempic face" + body laxity; (4) wedding/event; (5) competitive job market / dating market; (6) cancer reconstruction post-mastectomy; (7) traumatic injury / scar revision; (8) gender affirming.

## 2. Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: Mommy Makeover (28%).** 32–48, 1–3 kids, $120k–$280k HHI, post-breastfeeding. JTBD: "Get my body back." Verbatim: "I'm done having kids." Fear: scarring, anesthesia, time off from kids. Winning: BC surgeon, virtual consult option, financing.

**Persona 2: Aesthetic Younger (22%).** 24–35, $80k–$200k HHI, single or bridal. JTBD: "Breast aug / rhino / lip lift." Fear: looking fake, looking different. Winning: subtle results portfolio.

**Persona 3: Post-GLP-1 (18%).** 35–55, lost 50–120 lb on Ozempic/Mounjaro/Wegovy, loose skin. JTBD: "Body lift / tummy tuck / facial volume restoration." Winning: GLP-1-specific protocols.

**Persona 4: Facial Anti-Aging (16%).** 45–65, $200k+ HHI. JTBD: "Deep-plane facelift, blepharoplasty." Fear: pulled, wind-tunnel look. Winning: natural results, deep-plane vs SMAS education.

**Persona 5: Reconstructive Cross-Over (10%).** Post-mastectomy / trauma. JTBD: "Reconstruction with cosmetic finesse." Often insurance-covered.

**Persona 6: Male (6%).** 35–55, gyno / liposuction / hair / chin implant. JTBD: "Subtle masculine improvements." Winning: male-specialized portfolio.

- **O3 — "How much downtime?"** Rebuttal: "Procedure-specific: breast 1 wk light, tummy tuck 2–4 wks."
- **O4 — "I want a specific look — will it be natural?"** Rebuttal: "Computer simulation + photo morph in consult."
- **O5 — "Scarring?"** Rebuttal: "Incision-minimized technique + silicone scar protocol."
- **O6 — "I'm scared of anesthesia."** Rebuttal: "Anesthesiologist (not CRNA) on every case."
- **O7 — "What if I don't like it?"** Rebuttal: "Touch-up policy in writing."

- **O10 — "I want to wait."** Rebuttal: "Surgery dates are 8–14 weeks out — book consult to hold."

## 11. Prohibited Claims

Banned: "guaranteed result," "no scarring," "no pain," "100% safe," "permanent results" (most aren't), "FDA approved" misused (FDA approves devices, not procedures), "best surgeon in [city]" without verifiable award, before/afters without HIPAA auth, testimonials without auth, BBL safety overpromise (BBL has elevated mortality vs other procedures), "completely natural" (puffery).

## 12. Compliance Rules

**HIPAA Privacy / Security / Marketing Rule (45 CFR §164):** strict. Photos require written authorization. **HHS Online Tracking Tech (2023, 2024):** Pixel on patient pages = breach risk. **State Medical Board:** ABPS / ABFPRS recognized; "Board-Certified Plastic Surgeon" should be reserved for ABPS. **FDA Breast Implant Labeling (2021):** Black Box warning + patient checklist required pre-implant. **BIA-ALCL & BII (Breast Implant Illness):** disclosure standards evolving. **TCPA / CAN-SPAM / FTC §5.** **FTC Endorsement Guides (16 CFR §255):** influencer #ad. **State CPOM doctrine** (CA / NY / NJ / TX / IL): cosmetic-only practice ownership must be MD. **State surgical center licensing.** **Anesthesia + OR accreditation (AAAASF / Joint Commission / AAAHC):** required by many states for office-based surgery >X anesthesia level. **OIG / Stark for any insurance-billed reconstruction.** **ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA.**
