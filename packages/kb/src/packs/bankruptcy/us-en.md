## 1. Market Overview

US consumer bankruptcy filings projected ~520k in 2025 (rising 12% YoY off post-pandemic lows; cycle-sensitive) [industry estimate based on US Courts Statistics + ABI 2025]. Market size $4.8B in attorney fees. Average filing fee revenue: $1,200–$2,500 Chapter 7 (no-asset); $3,500–$4,800 Ch 7 with assets; $4,500–$6,500 Chapter 13 (paid through plan over 3–5 yrs); $9,500–$24,000 small business Subchapter V Chapter 11.

Buying triggers: (1) wage garnishment served (urgent); (2) foreclosure / repo imminent; (3) credit card sued (judgment); (4) medical debt overwhelming; (5) divorce + debt; (6) small business failing; (7) student loan + private loan strangulation; (8) tax debt panic. **2024–2025 rising credit card delinquency** drives demand.

- **O3 — "Will I lose my house / car?"** Rebuttal: "Federal + state exemptions protect equity; reaffirmation for car."
- **O4 — "What about my retirement?"** Rebuttal: "ERISA-protected 401k/IRA exempt up to $1.5M+ (2024 update)."
- **O5 — "I'm embarrassed."** Rebuttal: "Confidential; 520k Americans file per year."
- **O6 — "Will my boss know?"** Rebuttal: "Garnishment is more public than filing; filing actually stops garnishment."
- **O7 — "Tax debt?"** Rebuttal: "Income tax 3+ yrs old, return filed, no fraud = often dischargeable."
- **O8 — "Sued by credit card?"** Rebuttal: "Automatic stay halts lawsuit upon filing."

- **O10 — "Can I file alone?"** Rebuttal: "Pro se possible but risky — 80%+ of pro se Ch 7 are dismissed or trustee-objected."

## 12. Compliance Rules

**ABA Model Rules 7.1–7.5 + state analogues.** **Bankruptcy Code 11 USC §526–528 (Debt Relief Agency rules):** specific advertising disclosures required, including "We are a debt relief agency. We help people file for bankruptcy relief under the Bankruptcy Code." This disclosure is mandatory on all bankruptcy ads, websites, and form letters. Must be conspicuous. **§527/528 written disclosures** at engagement. **BAPCPA (2005)** means test rules. **TCPA / CAN-SPAM / FTC §5.** **FTC + state AG actions** against debt-relief scams require care with marketing. **CFPB** has authority over debt-relief companies. **State Bar advertising filing** + specialist rule. **No fee-sharing with non-lawyer debt-relief.** **Trust accounting (IOLTA).** **ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA.**
