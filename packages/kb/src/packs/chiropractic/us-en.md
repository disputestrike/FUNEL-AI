## 1. Market Overview

US chiropractic services market is $19.4B in 2025, growing 3–5% YoY [industry estimate based on ACA / IBISWorld 2025]. Approximately 70,000 active DCs across 38,000 practice locations. Average new-patient lifetime value: $1,800 for cash-based wellness; $4,800 for insurance-billed neck/back; $6,200 for PI/auto cases; $2,200 for decompression / non-surgical disc programs. Recurring monthly maintenance: $80–$200/mo membership.

Sales cycle: same-day to 7 days for symptom-driven; 14–30 days for wellness conversions. Gross margin: 55–72% on cash-based; 40–55% insurance-billed. Acceptable CAC: $48–$180 per new patient. Acceptable CPL: $14–$58.

Buying triggers: (1) acute back pain; (2) sciatica; (3) post-MVA (motor vehicle accident); (4) chronic headaches; (5) athletic injury; (6) pregnancy-related; (7) post-surgical alternative; (8) wellness / posture-driven; (9) insurance benefit reset Jan 1. Decompression / non-surgical disc has high deal value but is regulated.

## 2. Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: Acute Back Pain Walk-In (38%).** 30–60, hurt last 2 weeks, never been to chiro. JTBD: "Stop the pain without surgery or opioids." Verbatim: "I threw my back out moving the couch." Fear: cracked-neck stories, scary adjustments. Winning: gentle technique options (Activator), free initial consult.

**Persona 2: MVA / Post-Accident PI Case (22%).** Recently in car accident. JTBD: "Document injury for claim + treat." Winning: PI attorney relationship, LOP (Letter of Protection), documentation.

**Persona 3: Chronic Pain Sufferer (18%).** 45–70, multi-year. JTBD: "Manage chronic pain without daily meds." Winning: integrated multi-modal (chiro + massage + PT + decompression).

**Persona 4: Wellness Maintenance (14%).** 35–60, $120k+ HHI. JTBD: "Stay aligned; preventive." Winning: monthly membership.

**Persona 5: Athlete (8%).** 18–45. JTBD: "Performance + recovery." Winning: ART (Active Release), recovery modalities.

## 5. Common Objections

- **O1 — "Once you go, you have to go forever."** Rebuttal: "Myth. We treat symptoms then graduate you. You decide on maintenance."
- **O2 — "Cracking necks is dangerous."** Rebuttal: "We offer Activator (no manual cracking) if you prefer."
- **O3 — "Is it covered by insurance?"** Rebuttal: "We verify your benefits before visit."
- **O4 — "I'd rather see a real doctor."** Rebuttal: "Chiropractic is a doctorate. We co-refer with PCPs and MDs."
- **O5 — "I tried it once and it didn't help."** Rebuttal: "Single visit rarely resolves chronic. Plan-based is the standard of care."
- **O6 — "Too expensive."** Rebuttal: "Self-pay bundles + financing."
- **O7 — "I'd rather just take ibuprofen."** Rebuttal: "Long-term NSAIDs have GI and kidney risk. Mechanical fix is sustainable."
- **O8 — "I'm pregnant — safe?"** Rebuttal: "Webster-certified, prenatal-trained."
- **O9 — "I had a car accident — do I see a chiro or PI lawyer first?"** Rebuttal: "Both. We document; lawyer pursues claim."
- **O10 — "Too far / no time."** Rebuttal: "Early 7am + Saturday slots."

## 12. Compliance Rules

**HIPAA Privacy / Security / Marketing Rules (45 CFR §164):** PHI marketing requires authorization. Before/after photos require auth. **State Board of Chiropractic Examiners:** each state regulates ads (e.g., CA Board of Chiropractic Examiners §312; FL §456.072; TX TBCE §76). Many states ban "free" exams when bundled with billable services (FL strict; AZ strict). **TCPA / CAN-SPAM / FTC §5.** **FTC Health Products Compliance Guidance (2022):** substantiate. **FTC has filed actions against chiropractors for COVID-19 / cancer / Lyme claims** (e.g., 2020 FTC warning letters). **OIG Anti-Kickback Statute** for PI attorney relationships — no fee-splitting; LOP arrangements must be properly structured. **ACA Section 1557** nondiscrimination. **HHS Online Tracking Tech guidance (2023/2024):** Pixel on patient pages = HIPAA risk. **State licensing display required.** **ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA.**

## 22. Pitfalls

(1) FTC COVID/Lyme/cancer claims = lawsuit. (2) Meta Pixel on patient page = HIPAA. (3) PI fee-splitting = OIG violation. (4) Free exam with billable upsell — state board violation in FL/AZ/etc. (5) "Cure" / "miracle" language. (6) No HIPAA auth on testimonials. (7) Skipping TCPA consent. (8) Promising insurance coverage. (9) "Specialist" without state-allowed credential. (10) Crossing into PT/MD scope of practice.
