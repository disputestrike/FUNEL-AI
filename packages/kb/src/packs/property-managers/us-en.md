# GoFunnelAI KB Pack — Property Management (US-EN)

> Pack ID: `property-managers-us-en` | Vertical: Residential property management (SFH + small multi + STR / Airbnb) | Geo: US | Language: EN

## 1. Market Overview

US residential property management market is $108B in 2025 [industry estimate based on IBISWorld + NAA 2025]. Approximately 23M rental units (44% of which are professionally managed). PM fee revenue split: long-term rentals 8–10% of monthly rent + leasing fee 50–100% of one month's rent + setup fee; short-term rentals 18–28% of nightly revenue.

Average PM revenue per door: long-term $90–$240/mo; STR $480–$1,800/mo (gross). Sales cycle: 14–60 days for owner acquisition. Acceptable CAC: $400–$1,400 per new door. Acceptable CPL: $32–$140 at 8–20% close.

Buying triggers: (1) DIY landlord burnout; (2) accidental landlord (couldn't sell); (3) cross-state owner; (4) new investor buying first property; (5) eviction crisis (tenant non-pay); (6) maintenance overwhelm; (7) STR transition or compliance change (NYC Local Law 18, Austin caps, etc.); (8) vacancy stress; (9) rent-control change.

## 2. Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: Accidental Landlord (32%).** 35–60, moved for job, can't sell, renting current home. JTBD: "Manage from distance; don't get burned." Verbatim: "I never wanted to be a landlord." Fear: bad tenant, eviction. Winning: thorough screening, on-the-ground manager.

**Persona 2: Cross-State Investor (24%).** 38–60, $200k+ HHI, 1–8 SFH portfolio out-of-state. JTBD: "Hands-off PM; monthly statements; tax-ready 1099." Winning: owner portal, transparent reporting.

**Persona 3: DIY Burnout (18%).** Was self-managing 1–5 units, ready to pay. JTBD: "Stop fielding tenant calls at 11pm." Winning: 24/7 maintenance dispatch.

**Persona 4: STR Owner (14%).** Airbnb/VRBO portfolio. JTBD: "Maximize ADR + occupancy + compliance." Winning: dynamic-pricing + permit handling.

**Persona 5: Small-Multi Investor (12%).** Duplexes / 4-plexes. JTBD: "Scale efficiently." Winning: per-door pricing, bulk discount.

## 12. Compliance Rules

**Fair Housing Act (42 USC §3601)** strictly enforced in ads. HUD aggressively investigates discriminatory listings. **State Fair Housing extensions** (CA Source-of-Income, NY rent stab tenant rights). **State landlord-tenant statutes:** CA Civil Code §1940+; FL §83; TX Property Code §92; NY RPL §232. **Security deposit rules:** state-specific. **Eviction process:** state-specific including just-cause states (CA, NJ, OR, WA). **Trust account separation** required by state real estate commission. **State broker licensure for PM:** all states require RE broker license to manage for others (some exempt for single owner / small portfolios). **NARPM Code of Ethics.** **TCPA / CAN-SPAM / FTC §5.** **GLBA** for tenant payment data. **PCI-DSS** for rent payment processing. **STR Local laws:** NYC Local Law 18 (2023) — host must register, occupancy limits; Austin caps; LA STR rules; Nashville. **Occupancy tax (TOT):** state + local. **ADA / WCAG 2.1 AA.**

## 22. Pitfalls

(1) "No Section 8" in source-of-income protected states. (2) Fair Housing language slippage. (3) Trust account commingling. (4) Skipping state broker license. (5) Vendor kickback (RESPA §8 doesn't apply but state PM laws do). (6) Pixel + GLBA collision. (7) STR ignored local law (huge fines NYC). (8) Eviction without proper notice → wrongful eviction. (9) Just-cause violation in CA/NJ/OR/WA. (10) Security deposit return outside state-specific deadline.
