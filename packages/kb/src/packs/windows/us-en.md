## 1. Market Overview

US residential replacement window market is $12.4B in 2025 (replacement only; new construction excluded), growing 4–6% YoY [industry estimate based on Window & Door Manufacturers Association + Statista 2025]. Approximately 21M residential windows are replaced per year across ~3.4M homes. Average job size: $9,200 for a typical 8-window vinyl whole-home project; $14,800 for fiberglass or composite; $24,000+ for premium wood-clad (Andersen, Marvin, Pella ProLine); $42,000+ for premium with full-frame replacement and trim restoration. Per-window typical: $650–$1,400 installed.

Gross margin: 38–52% on vinyl, 28–38% on premium clad-wood, 50%+ on insert/pocket installs. Sales cycle: 7–14 days from first touch to signed contract on retail; 21+ on premium custom; same-day close common with in-home demo + first-call discount. Customer LTV is mostly single-transaction (~1.1 lifetime jobs per home). Acceptable CAC: $500–$1,400 per close. Acceptable CPL: $60–$180 at 6–11% close.

Buying triggers: (1) drafty rooms / high energy bills; (2) failed seals / fogging between panes; (3) HOA notice of paint peeling on wood; (4) post-storm damage; (5) historic-property renovation; (6) new homeowner first winter; (7) IRA 25C Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit (up to $600/year for ENERGY STAR windows); (8) state weatherization rebates; (9) noise mitigation (highway-adjacent); (10) hurricane-impact glass requirements (FL, coastal).

## 2. Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: Energy-Bill Replacer (44%).** 45–70, single-family, 15–30 years in the home, original windows. JTBD: "Lower drafts, lower bill, modern look." Verbatim: "feels like the heat is leaving through the windows." Fear: getting upsold from $500/window to $1,800/window. Winning: written warranty (lifetime non-pro vinyl), ENERGY STAR rating, locally-made trust play. **Persona 2: Fog/Failed-Seal Replacer (22%).** Visible fogging between panes. JTBD: "Just replace the bad ones — maybe more later." Risk: contractor pressures whole-home. **Persona 3: Curb-Appeal Upgrader (18%).** 35–55, $200k+ HHI, pre-sale or aesthetic. JTBD: "Black aluminum-look casement frames, premium glass." **Persona 4: Hurricane-Impact Requirement (10%).** FL/TX/coastal Carolinas/AL/MS — building code requires impact-rated. JTBD: "Code-compliant, insurance-qualified impact glass." **Persona 5: Historic-Property Owner (6%).** Wants wood-clad with simulated divided lites, period-correct.

## 5. Common Objections

- **O1 — "Your price is twice the other quote."** Rebuttal: "The other quote is probably a lower-spec vinyl or insert-only. Let me show you the per-window comparison."
- **O2 — "I need to think."** Rebuttal: "Specifically what?"
- **O3 — "I want to compare 3 estimates."** Rebuttal: "Smart. Here's our written warranty and ENERGY STAR ratings — most quotes don't include those."
- **O4 — "I'll wait for spring."** Rebuttal: "Lead times push from 4 weeks to 12 in spring. And IRA credit resets Jan 1."
- **O5 — "I don't need all of them — just 2."** Rebuttal: "Fine. We do 2 — your call. Most customers do partial first, then back-fill later."
- **O6 — "I read bad reviews about [brand]."** Rebuttal: "What specifically? Often it's an install issue, not the brand. Let me address it."
- **O7 — "Warranty is meaningless."** Rebuttal: "Our manufacturer non-prorated lifetime is transferable. Most aren't."
- **O8 — "What about my historic trim?"** Rebuttal: "We do pocket installs that preserve original trim."
- **O9 — "Lead times?"** Rebuttal: "4–6 weeks build, 1–2 days install."
- **O10 — "What if my window doesn't fit?"** Rebuttal: "We measure twice, manufacturer makes to spec, written warranty on fit."

## 12. Compliance Rules

**TCPA:** one-to-one consent. **CAN-SPAM.** **FTC Act §5 + 16 CFR §251 (Free).** **State contractor licensing:** CA C-17/B; FL CGC/CRC; TX no state but municipal; NY varies. License # on every ad. **FTC Funeral Rule N/A; FTC Cooling-Off Rule (16 CFR Part 429)** applies to in-home sales — 3-day right of rescission must be given in writing. **State home-improvement contractor acts** (e.g., MD HICA, PA HICPA, NY GBL §770): contract content requirements, license display, deposit caps. **ENERGY STAR / NFRC** ratings must be cited accurately. **FL Product Approval / TX TDI / AAMA Gold Label** displayable. **HUD Lead Paint Disclosure** (pre-1978 homes) — RRP rule (40 CFR Part 745) applies to renovation including window replacement; install crew must be EPA RRP certified. **ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA.**

## 22. Pitfalls

(1) Skipping the 3-day Cooling-Off Rule notice in writing — voids the contract. (2) Not EPA RRP certified on pre-1978 homes. (3) Vague lifetime warranty. (4) Quoting without measure (callbacks). (5) Misstating IRA credit ($600 ANNUAL, not lifetime). (6) "Hurricane proof" instead of "impact rated." (7) Door-knocking aftermath of storm. (8) Buying shared leads. (9) "Free windows" anywhere. (10) Not displaying license # on creative.
