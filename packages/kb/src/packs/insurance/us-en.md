## 1. Market Overview

US insurance distribution is $1.6T+ in premium volume in 2025 across life ($165B), Medicare Advantage / Supplement ($455B), individual health ($95B), P&C personal lines ($380B), and final expense ($14B+) [industry estimate based on LIMRA + AHIP + III 2025]. Agent commission income market: $98B+. Approximately 1.1M licensed producers in the US.

Average per-policy commission: term life $300–$1,200 first year + 5–10% renewal; whole life $1,500–$8,000 first year + renewal; Medicare Advantage $611 first year + $306 annual renewal (CMS-set 2025 cap); Medicare Supp similar; final expense $400–$1,200 first year; ACA individual $200–$500 first year + renewal; auto $80–$400 first year + 10–14% renewal. Top producers earn $200k–$700k annually.

Sales cycle: same-day (Medicare AEP impulse) to 21 days (life with underwriting). Acceptable CAC: $80–$320. Acceptable CPL: $14–$95.

Buying triggers: (1) turning 65 (Medicare T-65); (2) Medicare AEP Oct 15–Dec 7; (3) ACA Open Enrollment Nov 1–Jan 15; (4) life event (baby, marriage, mortgage); (5) layoff / lost employer coverage; (6) auto rate hike non-renewal; (7) home purchase; (8) DUI / driving record change; (9) final expense (senior + family).

## 2. Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: Medicare-Aging-In (T-65) (32%).** Turning 65 in next 6 months. JTBD: "Pick the right Medicare path — MA vs Supplement + Part D." Verbatim: "I'm so confused — what's MA vs Medigap?" Fear: making a mistake during one-time IEP window. Winning: free comparison, plan ratings.

**Persona 2: Medicare AEP Shopper (22%).** Existing Medicare, shopping during Oct 15–Dec 7. JTBD: "Find a better plan." Winning: AEP-only attention.

**Persona 3: Life Insurance Buyer (16%).** 28–45, new baby / mortgage. JTBD: "Term to cover mortgage + kids." Fear: medical exam, denied for condition. Winning: no-exam options, accelerated underwriting.

**Persona 4: ACA Marketplace Shopper (14%).** Self-employed or laid off, $30k–$140k HHI. JTBD: "Affordable health coverage with subsidy." Winning: subsidy-eligible plan comparison.

**Persona 5: P&C Switcher (10%).** Got non-renewed (FL/CA fire/hurricane) or rate-shocked. JTBD: "Find a new carrier." Winning: multi-carrier independent.

**Persona 6: Final Expense (6%).** 50–80, family-driven. JTBD: "Cover funeral so kids don't have to." Winning: simplified-issue.

## 11. Prohibited Claims

Banned: For Medicare, anything not on CMS-approved marketing — must include CMS marketing disclaimer "We do not offer every plan available in your area." Cannot use Medicare logo, Medicare name in misleading way, "endorsed by Medicare," any government implication. Cannot promise specific savings. Cannot use SOA (scope of appointment) to discuss benefits not pre-agreed. For Life: cannot promise guaranteed issue without conditions. For ACA: cannot say "free healthcare" — subsidies vary. For P&C: cannot promise specific savings amount.

**Hard-block exact strings (CMS + state DOI + FTC):**
- `"government Medicare program"` (without clarification)
- `"endorsed by Medicare"`
- `"endorsed by CMS"`
- `"Medicare.gov official agent"`
- `"the only Medicare option"`
- `"free Medicare for life"`
- `"free Medicare Advantage"` (MA premiums vary; technically $0 plans exist but framing is regulated)
- `"new Medicare benefit"` (commonly used in deceptive ads)
- `"Medicare flex card"` (commonly misused for OTC card)
- `"government health insurance"` (without specifying program)
- `"Biden insurance plan"`
- `"Trump insurance plan"`
- `"new government insurance program"`
- `"guaranteed lowest auto rate"`
- `"guaranteed coverage no health questions"` (without naming specific guaranteed-issue context)
- `"qualify before midnight"`
- `"act before midnight"`
- `"100% free health insurance"`
- `"government will pay your premium"`

## 12. Compliance Rules

**CMS Medicare Marketing Guidelines (Chapter 4 + 2024 Final Rule + 2025 updates):** Scope of Appointment (SOA) required 48-hr before in-person enrollment meeting; recorded calls; required disclaimers; no door-to-door without prior consent; no unsolicited contact; no business cards left at unsolicited locations; permitted marketing materials must be filed with CMS via HPMS; agent/broker disclosure mandatory ("We do not offer every plan available in your area..."). **TPMO (Third-Party Marketing Organization) rule:** TPMO ads require disclaimer + plans must be vetted. **TCPA:** Medicare especially — one-to-one consent under FCC 2025 rule. **State DOI advertising regulations:** vary; many require pre-filing of life ads. **NAIC Model Replacement Regulation** for life. **Producer licensing** in every state agent solicits. **HIPAA** for health info collected. **Anti-Kickback / Stark** does not apply (private market). **State insurance fraud statutes.** **ACA Navigator vs Agent distinction.** **ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA.**
