## 11. Prohibited Claims

## 12. Compliance Rules

**TCPA / CAN-SPAM / FTC §5** standard. **FIFRA (Federal Insecticide, Fungicide, and Rodenticide Act, 7 USC §136)** — every applied product must be EPA-registered and used per label. **State pesticide licensing:** every state requires a Commercial Applicator license (e.g., CA DPR; TX TDA; FL FDACS; NY DEC). Display license # on every ad. **State Structural Pest Control Boards** (CA SPCB; TX SPCS; FL DACS) regulate advertising — most prohibit "guarantee" without written terms. **EPA label law:** label is the law; off-label use is a federal violation. **HUD Bed Bug rules** for multifamily. **WDIR / NPMA-33** form is regulated for real-estate transactions. **Endangered Species Act** affects rodenticide use in some counties. **ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA.**

## 22. Pitfalls

(1) "EPA approved" instead of "registered." (2) Off-label product use (federal violation). (3) Promising "100% elimination forever." (4) Skipping state-required notice to neighbors (CA SPCB). (5) Bed bug claims without heat documentation. (6) Termite warranty without proper bond/transferability. (7) Rodenticide use near endangered species habitats. (8) Vague "natural/organic" claims without OMRI. (9) Skipping applicator license display. (10) Buying shared leads (TCPA one-to-one rule).
