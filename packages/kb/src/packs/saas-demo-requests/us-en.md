# GoFunnelAI KB Pack — B2B SaaS Demo Requests (US-EN)

> Pack ID: `saas-demo-requests-us-en` | Vertical: B2B SaaS sales-led growth, demo-to-close pipelines | Geo: US | Language: EN

## 1. Market Overview

US B2B SaaS market is $390B in 2025 [industry estimate based on Gartner + IDC 2025]. SaaS demo-request marketing applies across horizontal (sales tools, marketing tools, ops tools, HR, finance, security) and vertical (real-estate SaaS, dental SaaS, restaurant SaaS, healthcare SaaS) categories. ACV ranges: $4k–$120k SMB; $48k–$680k mid-market; $250k–$5M+ enterprise. Average ACV across SDR-driven funnels: $24k SMB, $96k MM.

Sales cycle: 14–45 days SMB; 60–120 days mid-market; 90–270 days enterprise. LTV: 3.4× ACV (avg net-revenue retention) over 3.4 years. Acceptable CAC: $1.5k–$8k SMB; $12k–$48k mid-market. Acceptable CPL (MQL → SQL/demo): $60–$420 SMB / $180–$1,200 mid-market at 8–22% MQL-to-demo.

Buying triggers: (1) "switch from spreadsheets" (TAM-wide pain); (2) team scaling (>10 reps, >50 employees); (3) competitor renewal coming up; (4) integration need (Salesforce, HubSpot, Workday, etc.); (5) compliance event (SOC 2, HIPAA, GDPR); (6) leadership change; (7) board-level KPI mandate; (8) funding round → growth pressure.

## 2. Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: VP / Director Champion (38%).** 32–48, $180k–$350k. JTBD: "Find a tool that solves [X] for my team, gets exec sign-off." Verbatim: "I need a demo by Thursday — exec wants a recommendation by next week." Winning: prepared exec deck, clear ROI, fast turnaround.

**Persona 2: End-User Practitioner (24%).** 26–42. JTBD: "Get tool to make my daily job easier." Winning: self-serve trial, intuitive UX.

**Persona 3: Operations / Procurement (16%).** 32–55. JTBD: "Vet vendor — security, contract, pricing." Winning: SOC 2, MSA, transparent pricing.

**Persona 4: Executive Buyer (CEO/CFO/CRO) (12%).** 38–62. JTBD: "Justify investment with ROI / business case." Winning: business case template.

**Persona 5: Security / IT (10%).** 30–55. JTBD: "Pass SecRev / IT review." Winning: pre-emptive security packet.

## 11. Prohibited Claims

Banned: false competitor claims (defamation / trademark / Lanham Act); "we beat [competitor] every time"; fake customer logos; fictional case studies; "SOC 2 compliant" if cert lapsed (use "currently certified" with date); "HIPAA compliant" — HIPAA does not "certify" software; "GDPR compliant" with no DPA; fake free-trial.

**Hard-block exact strings:**
- `"HIPAA certified"` (HIPAA does not certify)
- `"HIPAA approved"` (HHS does not approve)
- `"FedRAMP certified"` (without actual ATO)
- `"SOC 2 certified"` (use: "SOC 2 Type II audited")
- `"GDPR certified"` (no certification body for GDPR)
- `"PCI certified"` (use: "PCI DSS Level X validated")
- `"#1 [category]"` (without verifiable basis)
- `"best in class"` (without basis)
- `"fastest in market"` (without basis)
- `"better than [competitor]"` (defamation risk)
- `"[competitor] is broken"` (defamation)
- `"[competitor] users hate it"` (defamation)
- `"100% uptime"` (no system achieves 100%)
- `"unhackable"` (false)
- `"government approved SaaS"`
- `"FBI uses our software"` (without permission)
- `"as featured in Gartner Magic Quadrant"` (without actual placement)
- `"AI-powered"` without basis (FTC scrutinizing AI claims in 2024–2025)
- `"act before midnight"`
- `"last day to lock in this rate"` (if false)

## 12. Compliance Rules

**FTC Act §5:** truthful comparative advertising. **Lanham Act §43(a) (15 USC §1125)** for false advertising affecting commerce. **Trademark law (15 USC §1114):** competitor names allowed in nominative fair use but with care. **CCPA / CPRA / GDPR / state privacy:** data collection on forms; cookie consent. **TCPA** for SMS/voice outreach. **CAN-SPAM** for email sequences. **FTC AI Compliance (2024 guidance):** "AI" claims must be substantiated; explainable; no deceptive AI capability claims (FTC has filed cases). **Telephone Consumer Protection Act + state DNC** for BDR / SDR outbound. **SOC 2 (AICPA TSC):** Type I = point in time; Type II = period. **HIPAA Privacy/Security Rules:** if healthcare-adjacent. **State Data Breach Notification laws.** **GDPR Article 28 DPA** required for processor relationships. **Schrems II / Data Privacy Framework** for EU-US transfers. **ADA / WCAG 2.1 AA** for product UI + marketing site.
