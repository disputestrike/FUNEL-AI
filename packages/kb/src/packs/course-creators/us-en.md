## 1. Market Overview

US online learning + creator-economy course market is $24B in 2025, growing 14–18% YoY [industry estimate based on Coursera + Teachable + Kajabi + ResearchAndMarkets 2025]. Average creator revenue: top 10% earn $300k+; median active seller $24k. Price points: $97–$497 self-paced; $497–$2,997 cohort-based; $1,997–$9,997 implementation programs; $27–$97/mo membership; $7–$47 tripwire.

Sales cycle: 3 days self-paced impulse; 14–45 days cohort cycle. LTV: $480 self-paced; $2,400 cohort; $1,800/yr membership. Acceptable CAC: $48–$320. Acceptable CPL: $4–$48 at 2–8% close (low-ticket) and 5–18% (mid-ticket).

Buying triggers: (1) skill-acquisition urgency (job market); (2) side-hustle interest; (3) creator inspired by other creator success; (4) AI-skill panic (2024–2026 ChatGPT-driven re-skilling); (5) industry shift (e.g., real estate post-NAR-settlement); (6) New Year resolution; (7) refund threat for prior bad course; (8) employer-paid PD budget Q4.

## 2. Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: Skill Seeker (40%).** 24–48, $60k–$160k HHI, wants skill (copy, design, code, AI). JTBD: "Get hireable skill fast." Fear: cheap content, no completion. Winning: short modules + community + outcomes.

**Persona 2: Side-Hustle Aspirant (24%).** 28–45, FT job, wants income side. JTBD: "Build $1–3k/mo side biz." Fear: scam. Winning: clear blueprint + tools list.

**Persona 3: Creator Studying Creator (16%).** 25–45, already running content. JTBD: "Scale audience / monetize." Winning: tactical, niche-specific.

**Persona 4: Pro Continuing Ed (12%).** 30–55, employer PD. JTBD: "Cert + skill for promotion." Winning: cert + LinkedIn-credential.

**Persona 5: Hobbyist (8%).** Cooking, photography, fitness. JTBD: "Improve at hobby." Lower urgency.

## 11. Prohibited Claims

Banned per FTC: typical-results disclosure required; income claims must be substantiated and typical-results disclosed; "guaranteed [X] outcome" needs basis; fake scarcity / countdown; "as seen on" without specific. Webinar claimed "live" when evergreen is FTC violation.

**Hard-block exact strings:**
- `"guaranteed $10k/month"`
- `"make $X in [time]"` (without typical-results)
- `"100% guaranteed"`
- `"#1 course in"`
- `"best course in"`
- `"government grant for course"`
- `"FTC approved"`
- `"miracle method"`
- `"secret system"`
- `"act before midnight"`
- `"only X spots left"` (if not actually limited)
- `"closing forever"` (if not actually closing)
- `"price doubles tomorrow"` (if false)
- `"as seen on Forbes"` (without actual mention)
- `"as featured in Inc"` (without basis)
- `"limited to first 100"` (if false)
- `"this opportunity will never come back"`
- `"unbeatable"`
- `"one-of-a-kind never repeated"`
- `"guaranteed transformation"`

## 12. Compliance Rules

**FTC Endorsement Guides (16 CFR §255):** typical results required; material connection disclosed. **FTC Income Claims Guidance (2023):** substantiation required + typical-results disclosure; "results not typical" alone insufficient. **FTC ROSCA (15 USC §8401):** recurring billing must have clear disclosure + easy cancellation. **FTC Negative Option Rule / Click-to-Cancel (2024):** parity required. **FTC Made in USA Rule** if applicable. **CAN-SPAM** unsubscribe. **TCPA** one-to-one consent. **COPPA** if under-13 audience. **GDPR / CCPA / CPRA.** **Webinar "live" misrepresentation** = FTC §5 deception. **Affiliate disclosure** under FTC. **State consumer protection** statutes. **ADA / WCAG 2.1 AA.**
