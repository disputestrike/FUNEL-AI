# GoFunnelAI KB Pack — Residential Rooftop Solar (US-EN)

> Pack ID: `solar-us-en` | Vertical: Residential rooftop PV installation | Geo: United States | Language: English

## 1. Market Overview

The US residential rooftop solar market is a $32.4B revenue industry in 2025, projected to grow to roughly $41B by 2028 [industry estimate based on SEIA/Wood Mackenzie US Solar Market Insight, 2025]. Underlying volume is approximately 880,000 home installations per year, with the installed base passing 5.0 million homes. Average system size is 7.5 kW DC at a blended national installed price of about $3.30/W, producing an average gross deal size of $24,750 before adders (battery, MPU, re-roof) and $19,880 net of the 30% federal Residential Clean Energy Credit (IRC Section 25D). Top-of-funnel TAM per single mid-size installer doing 400–1,200 installs annually is $14M–$42M; year-one realistic SOM for a GoFunnelAI installer customer is $2M–$8M in net-new closed revenue.

Average deal size: $28,400 gross including a small battery, $42,000 with a 10 kWh+ battery, $52,000+ with battery and re-roof bundled. Gross margin per deal runs 18–28% for typical installers and 32–40% for premium-tier brands with proprietary equipment. Customer LTV is largely one-shot but with referral, monitoring upgrades, and battery add-ons reaches roughly $31,000 over seven years. Acceptable CAC is $1,800–$3,200; best-in-class operators run $1,400. Acceptable CPL is $55–$140; best-in-class hit $42.

Sales cycle: median 38 days first-touch to closed-won; range 7 days (cash buyer ready to act) to 180 days (consideration shoppers). The critical path: ad click → form → set appointment → site survey/virtual design → proposal → signed contract → HOA/permit/utility interconnection → install → PTO (Permission to Operate) → first bill comparison. Forty-one percent of stalled deals are due to a non-present decision-maker spouse at the first consultation.

Buying triggers (top 7): (1) summer electric bill shock (heat-wave + utility rate-case + intent-search clusters); (2) utility rate-case approval (PUC/PSC publishes 6–24% rate hike); (3) IRS Section 25D federal tax credit awareness (still 30% through 2032 under IRA); (4) state net-metering rule change (e.g., California NEM 3.0 cliff drove pull-forward demand); (5) new EV purchase (Tesla / Rivian / Ford F-150 Lightning owners install solar within 9 months of EV delivery 47% of the time); (6) recent home purchase (new homeowners install within 18 months at 3× the rate of long-tenure owners); (7) neighbor install (door-to-door canvassed neighborhoods convert at 3.1× the local baseline).

## 2. Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: Bill-Shock Boomer (38% of deal volume).** Demographics: 55–72, married, owns a single-family home worth $400k–$900k, household income $90k–$220k, lives in a sun-heavy state (AZ, FL, TX, NV, CA). Psychographics: financially conservative, retired or near-retirement, fixed-income anxiety, distrusts both utility companies and salespeople, watches local TV news, votes regularly, reads Costco Connection. JTBD: "Help me stop my electric bill from eating my retirement income, with zero risk and a known payback I can explain to my spouse." Verbatim phrases: "my bill went from $180 to $340 in two summers," "I don't want a lease, I want to own it," "what happens when you go out of business in 15 years?" Secret fear: getting scammed by a fly-by-night installer who disappears before the warranty matters. Winning looks like: cash or short-term loan, system covers 95–105% of usage, locked-in monthly payment lower than current utility bill. Disqualifiers: shaded roof, HOA hostile, planning to sell within 3 years.

**Persona 2: EV-Owner Engineer (24%).** Demographics: 32–48, married with kids, dual-income $180k–$400k, drives a Tesla/Rivian/Lightning, owns a $600k–$1.5M home, urban or near-urban, west coast or northeast. Psychographics: data-driven, reads EnergySage, lurks on r/solar, builds spreadsheets, distrusts sales calls, will reject any installer that won't share equipment SKUs and warranty PDFs upfront. JTBD: "Spec the optimal system for my EV charging + future battery + heat-pump load, with credible long-term performance data." Verbatim phrases: "what's your PVWatts assumption?" "give me the line-item BOM," "I'm not interested until I see the production guarantee language." Secret fear: overpaying or getting locked into bad equipment they'll regret in 10 years. Winning: detailed design with bifacial panels or premium microinverters, monitoring access, app-level visibility. Disqualifiers: any "free solar" or "no money down" lease pitch.

**Persona 3: Anxious New Homeowner (18%).** 28–40, just bought first home, $120k–$260k income, urgent feeling that bill is "too high" without baseline data, sees neighbors with panels, gets door-knocked. JTBD: "Lock in a stable energy cost that doesn't surprise me, ideally without a big up-front check." Verbatim: "the salesman told me I'd save $200/month — is that true?" "can I do this with no money down?" Fear: signing a 20-year lease they don't understand. Winning: financed deal with payment lower than current utility bill, simple monthly statement.

**Persona 4: Rate-Hike-Triggered Suburbanite (14%).** 40–60, average home, woke up after a utility published a rate case, googled "solar panels cost". JTBD: "Hedge against the next rate hike." Fear: technology obsolescence. Winning: standard 7–10 kW system, 25-year warranty.

**Persona 5: Green-Values Buyer (6%).** Coastal, college-educated, $150k+ HHI, willing to pay a small premium for US-made or low-carbon-footprint panels. JTBD: "Align my home with my values." Fear: greenwashing. Winning: domestic content, Made-in-USA modules.

## 3. Pain Points

- **P1 — Electric bill keeps rising and is unpredictable.** Highest-intensity pain across all personas; verbatim "$340 in July is insane." Hook framing: "Lock today's rate for 25 years."
- **P2 — Distrust of solar salespeople (door-to-door scams).** 71% of homeowners report having been pitched poorly. Hook: "No high-pressure sales. Get a quote without a salesman in your living room."
- **P3 — Confusion about financing (lease vs PPA vs loan vs cash).** Hook: "See your three real options side-by-side in 90 seconds."

- **P7 — Concern the panels won't generate as promised.** Hook: "Production guarantee — we pay you if your system underperforms."
- **P8 — Tax credit confusion (am I eligible, will it expire?).** Hook: "30% federal tax credit through 2032 — locked in by federal law."
- **P9 — Permitting/interconnection delays.** Hook: "Average 47 days click-to-PTO. We handle every permit."
- **P10 — Resale value uncertainty.** Hook: "Owned solar adds an average $15,000 to home sale price per Zillow 2024 data."

## 4. Urgency Triggers

Seasonal: (1) June–August bill-shock — outbound and paid spend yield 1.7× normal conversion; (2) Q4 tax-credit deadline urgency ("must install in tax year to claim 25D this filing"); (3) spring shoulder season (March–May) when permitting offices are least backed up.

Financial: (1) utility rate-case approval (PUC publishes 6–24% hike); (2) net-metering rule change (NEM 3.0 in CA pulled 8 months of demand forward); (3) federal tax credit step-down anxiety (currently 30% through 2032 then phases down).

Regulatory: (1) state net-metering modifications; (2) state utility commission decoupling rulings; (3) Inflation Reduction Act domestic content adders. State-specific: AZ APS/SRP/TEP rate cases; CA NEM 3.0 successor tariffs; FL utility-led anti-solar lobbying; TX ERCOT volatility; NY Con Edison delivery-rate hikes; MA SMART program enrollment windows; NJ SuSI program.

Competitive/supply: panel tariff shifts, microinverter shortages, battery incentive sunsetting (e.g., expiring state battery storage rebates).

## 5. Common Objections

- **O1 — "I need to think about it / talk to my spouse."** Rebuttal: "Totally understand. The reason we ask both decision-makers to be present at the first consult is exactly that — so you don't have to summarize 90 minutes of technical content to your spouse alone. Can we re-set the appointment for an evening when both of you are home?"
- **O2 — "How do I know you'll still be around in 25 years?"** Rebuttal: "Fair question. Our equipment manufacturer (REC/Qcells/Enphase) carries the 25-year product warranty independently — even if we vanished tomorrow, your panels are covered. Plus we've been operating locally since [year]."
- **O3 — "I heard solar leases are a rip-off."** Rebuttal: "Agreed — leases and PPAs are often bad deals because you don't own the asset or claim the tax credit. That's why we lead with cash and loan options where you own the system. Let me show you the side-by-side."
- **O4 — "It's too expensive."** Rebuttal: "Compared to what? Your 25-year utility bill at a 3.2% annual increase is $X. Our system, financed, replaces that with a known payment of $Y. The relevant question isn't 'is solar expensive' — it's 'is the next 25 years of utility payments more expensive than this loan.'"
- **O5 — "My roof is old."** Rebuttal: "Great that you brought that up. If your roof is over 15 years old we recommend re-roofing first or as part of the install — we can bundle and finance the whole project. Better to do it once."
- **O6 — "What if rates go down?"** Rebuttal: "Residential utility rates in the US have risen every single year since 1972 at an average of 3.4% [BLS CPI for electricity]. There is no scenario in modern history where they have fallen sustainably."
- **O7 — "I'm moving in a few years."** Rebuttal: "Owned solar adds an average $15,000 to home sale price per Zillow 2024 — and homes with solar sell 20% faster. Your payoff is at sale, not 25 years out."
- **O8 — "The tax credit doesn't apply to me."** Rebuttal: "Section 25D requires federal tax liability. If you owe federal taxes you can claim it; if you don't owe enough this year you can carry it forward. Let's check your liability."
- **O9 — "I want to wait for better panels."** Rebuttal: "Panel efficiency has gained about 0.5% per year. Waiting 5 years saves you about 2.5% efficiency — but costs you 5 years of utility bills, current tax credit, and current net metering."
- **O10 — "My neighbor's panels broke."** Rebuttal: "Sorry to hear it. Was that a lease/PPA from a national door-knocker? Those installs are notorious for poor workmanship. Our local crews are W-2 employees, not subcontractors. Let me show you our complaint record."

## 11. Prohibited Claims

Never claim or imply: (a) the system is "free" — solar is never free; (b) "no cost to you" without disclosing the financing instrument; (c) "the government pays for your solar" — the tax credit is not a government grant; (d) "guaranteed savings of $X" without a written production guarantee; (e) any rebate/incentive amount as fact without state-specific verification; (f) "limited time" if it's not actually limited; (g) "your utility is required to buy your power" without citing the state-specific net-metering rule.

## 12. Compliance Rules

**TCPA (47 USC §227):** No autodialer / prerecorded calls or texts to mobile numbers without prior express written consent. Form opt-in must include an explicit TCPA disclosure with a checkbox or unambiguous click-to-consent (FCC 2025 one-to-one consent rule — consent is to ONE seller, not a lead network). SMS must honor STOP/HELP. Voice calls must offer DNC option. Pre-recorded calls require separate written consent. Quiet hours 8 a.m.–9 p.m. local.

**CAN-SPAM (15 USC §7701):** Physical mailing address in every email footer; functional unsubscribe processed within 10 business days; no false subject lines.

**FTC Act §5:** No deceptive or unsubstantiated claims. Savings claims must have written substantiation. "Free" is governed by 16 CFR §251 — solar cannot be advertised as free.

**State licensing:** Most states require a C-46/C-10/EC-13 contractor license. Cite license number on every ad and landing page. CA: CSLB C-46 or C-10. AZ: ROC contractor classification. FL: CVC. TX: TDLR Electrical. NY: state + municipal.

**IRS Section 25D:** Tax credit claims must be accurate. Cannot state "you will receive a 30% rebate" — it is a nonrefundable credit against federal tax liability.

**FTC Solar Guide (2024):** Specific guidance on solar advertising — savings claims, ownership claims, tax claims must be substantiated.

**State consumer protection statutes:** CA Solar Consumer Protection Guide must be provided to the customer before contract signing. CT, MA, NJ have similar mandates. Failure to provide is a per-violation fine.

**ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA:** Required for ad creative and landing pages.

## 22. Pitfalls

(1) Running broad Meta interest targeting without homeowner filter — burns 40% of spend on renters. (2) Calling outside TCPA quiet hours. (3) Using "free solar" anywhere — instant ad disapproval + state AG risk. (4) Promising tax-credit dollar amounts without IRS substantiation. (5) Buying shared/co-reg leads from networks (FCC one-to-one consent rule). (6) Letting RevTry call delay >5 min — close rate falls off a cliff. (7) Not requiring both decision-makers at consult — 41% stall rate. (8) Generic creative without utility-name personalization. (9) Quoting NEM 3.0 incorrectly in CA. (10) Ignoring HOA approval workflow on funnels.
