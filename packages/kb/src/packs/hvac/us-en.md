## 1. Market Overview

US residential HVAC is a $89B annual market in 2025 (installation + service + parts), growing 5–7% YoY driven by aging equipment stock, federal heat-pump incentives under IRA Section 25C and 25D plus High-Efficiency Electric Home Rebate Act (HEEHRA), and 2023 DOE efficiency rule updates [industry estimate based on ACHR + ARI 2025]. Approximately 7M residential HVAC system replacements occur per year. Average ticket size: $8,400 for full system replacement (AC + furnace) at builder grade; $13,500 typical mid-tier (16 SEER2); $22,000–$38,000 for premium variable-speed heat pump with zoning; $180–$320 for a service call; $480 for a tune-up; $1,800 for a typical capacitor/blower/fan-motor repair.

Sales cycle: 3 days median first-touch to signed install on no-heat / no-cool emergency; 9–18 days for a planned replacement; 21+ for a heat pump conversion involving incentive paperwork. Gross margin: 28–42% on installs; 50–65% on service; 70%+ on maintenance memberships. Best-in-class operators run 22–34% net margin. LTV per customer with maintenance membership is $9,200 over 10 years. Acceptable CAC: $280–$650 (install lead); $40–$95 (service call). Acceptable CPL: $45–$120 install; $18–$45 service.

Buying triggers: (1) system failure mid-summer or mid-winter (highest urgency); (2) system age 12+ years and repeated repairs; (3) IRA 25C credit awareness ($2,000 cap on heat pump, $600 on AC, expires 2032); (4) HEEHRA point-of-sale rebate launch by state; (5) utility rebate cycles; (6) new homeowner first summer; (7) home renovation; (8) home sale prep; (9) refrigerant phaseout (R-410A → R-32/R-454B in 2025 created last-of-the-line urgency); (10) hot/cold spell forecast.

## 2. Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: Emergency Replacement Owner (38%).** 35–70, AC died yesterday, hot week, panicking. JTBD: "Get cool air today, financed if needed." Verbatim: "the tech said the compressor's shot, $4,500 to fix or $9k to replace." Fear: getting overcharged in panic. Winning: same-day install, financing, written diagnostic. **Persona 2: Planned Replacer (28%).** 45–70, system age 15+, getting two quotes. JTBD: "Don't get scammed; right-size the system; understand the SEER2 vs cost tradeoff." Winning: load calc (Manual J), written proposal, brand comparison. **Persona 3: Heat-Pump Curious Greenwise (16%).** 40–60, $150k+ HHI, cares about emissions and IRA credits. JTBD: "Right-size a cold-climate heat pump with backup heat if needed; capture every incentive." Winning: detailed incentive stack worksheet. **Persona 4: Service-Call Caller (12%).** Any age, just broken. JTBD: "Fix it cheap." **Persona 5: Maintenance-Plan Lifecycler (6%).** Past customer, signing annual plan.

## 4. Urgency Triggers

Seasonal: heat-wave alerts (NWS), cold-snap forecasts, shoulder-season pre-tune-up windows. Financial: IRA 25C $2k heat-pump credit (annual cap, resets), HEEHRA point-of-sale rebate (income-tested, state launch dates vary), utility rebates (e.g., SCE, ConEd, ComEd, PSE&G); refrigerant phase-out price increases (R-410A scarcity post-2025 install rule). Regulatory: 2023 DOE SEER2 minimum (14.3 SEER2 South, 13.4 SEER2 North); 2025 refrigerant transition; state-level natural-gas appliance bans (CA, MA, NY localities) accelerating heat-pump conversion.

## 5. Common Objections

- **O1 — "Your price is $3k higher than the other quote."** Rebuttal: "Let me compare the line items — SEER2 rating, install warranty, refrigerant type, electrical work included."
- **O2 — "I just want to fix it, not replace."** Rebuttal: "Totally fair. Here's the repair cost vs replacement break-even — if the unit is 12+ years and the repair is over 30% of replacement, replacement wins. Your call."
- **O3 — "Heat pumps don't work in cold."** Rebuttal: "True for the old ones. Cold-climate models (Mitsubishi Hyper-Heat, Bosch IDS) are rated to -15°F with no auxiliary heat needed. Let me show you."
- **O4 — "I want to wait until next year."** Rebuttal: "The IRA 25C credit caps at $2k per year and resets — but installation timelines back up in spring. Plus your current unit may not survive another summer."
- **O5 — "I don't trust the diagnostic."** Rebuttal: "I'll send you the photo of the compressor windings + Megger reading. Second opinion welcome — most won't offer that."
- **O6 — "I'm not sure I qualify for the rebate."** Rebuttal: "HEEHRA is income-tested — under 80% AMI is 100% covered up to caps, 80–150% is 50%. We'll check before quoting."
- **O7 — "I need to talk to my spouse."** Rebuttal: "Great. Most installs need both spouses' approval. Can we re-set when both of you are home?"
- **O8 — "What's your warranty?"** Rebuttal: "10-year parts via [Carrier/Trane/Lennox], 2-year labor by us, lifetime compressor on premium models."
- **O9 — "What if you go out of business?"** Rebuttal: "Manufacturer warranties are independent of us. Plus we've been local since [year]."
- **O10 — "I want to think."** Rebuttal: "Of course. Quick question — what specifically do you want to think through? Often I can answer."

## 12. Compliance Rules

**TCPA:** one-to-one consent; quiet hours; STOP/HELP. **CAN-SPAM.** **EPA Section 608:** refrigerant handling — only certified techs may handle refrigerant; selling to non-certified handlers is a federal violation. **DOE rules:** SEER2 / HSPF2 minimums by region. **IRA 25C / 25D:** credit amounts and eligibility must be accurately stated; cannot claim "rebate" when it's a nonrefundable credit. **HEEHRA:** rebate program is state-administered; must verify state launch and income tier before promising. **State licensing:** every state requires an HVAC contractor license (CA C-20; TX TDLR; FL CMC; NY varies by county). Display license # on every ad. **FTC Act §5.** **Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act** for warranty statements. **ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA.**
