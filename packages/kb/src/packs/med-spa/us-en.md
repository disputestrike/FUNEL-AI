US med-spa market is $24.6B in 2025, growing 12–15% YoY (highest growth in regulated health services) [industry estimate based on American Med Spa Association 2025]. Approximately 12,500 active med-spa locations. Average per-treatment ticket: Botox $480 (20 units at $24/u); Dysport / Xeomin / Daxxify similar; lip filler $750; cheek/jaw filler $1,200; PDO threads $1,800; PRP / PRF $850; Sculptra $1,400/vial × 2–4 sessions; CoolSculpting $1,500–$4,500 per area; Morpheus8 $2,500/session × 3; HydraFacial $250; chemical peel $200–$600; IPL $400; laser hair removal package $1,800.

Annual LTV per active customer: $3,800–$8,500 (membership patients $12k+). Gross margin: 65–78% on injectables; 50–62% on devices (after capital amortization). Sales cycle: same-day for first treatment; 14–60 days for body contouring high-ticket. Acceptable CAC: $80–$320. Acceptable CPL: $24–$98.

Buying triggers: (1) social-media trigger (Instagram, TikTok); (2) wedding/event; (3) divorce / new-relationship; (4) menopause; (5) post-pregnancy; (6) holiday season; (7) friend referral; (8) New Year resolution. Strong seasonality: Q4 highest, January #2.

## 12. Compliance Rules

**HIPAA Privacy / Security / Marketing Rules (45 CFR §164):** PHI marketing requires authorization. Before/afters require written auth. Pixel on patient pages = HIPAA risk per HHS 2023 guidance. **State Medical Board scope-of-practice:** who can inject varies — RN under physician delegation in most states; NP/PA broader scope; some states (e.g., NV, OR) require physician on-site. **Corporate Practice of Medicine doctrine:** med-spas in CA, NY, NJ, TX, IL, NC must be physician-owned (MD-only entity) — non-MD ownership of medical services is illegal. **FDA off-label promotion rules:** can use off-label, cannot promote off-label. **TCPA / CAN-SPAM / FTC §5.** **FTC Health Products Compliance Guidance (2022):** substantiate. **FTC Endorsement Guides (16 CFR §255):** influencer disclosure required; #ad. **OIG Anti-Kickback / Stark:** typically not implicated for cash med-spa but if billing insurance, applies. **State med-spa-specific laws:** FL §458.347 (delegation); CA B&P §2052; NY Public Health §238. **Allē / BD Aspire / Galderma ASPIRE** loyalty programs have advertising rules. **ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA.**
